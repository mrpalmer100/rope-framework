# Attribution and intellectual origin

The core idea developed here — that physical phenomena arise from a two-strand
rope-like structure connecting matter, with light as a transverse disturbance
along the rope, and the insistence that physical theories must describe *objects*
("things") rather than non-object *concepts* — originates with **Bill Gaede**
(the "Rope Hypothesis" / "Thread Theory"). The object-versus-concept distinction
that motivates this work (see [`philosophy.md`](philosophy.md)) is his.

This package is an **independent, modified, and mathematized development** of that
core idea, and it departs from Gaede's formulation in substantive ways — for
example, treating magnetism as a vortex structure rather than his jump-rope
picture, and adding a topological-charge formalism, an effective-field-theory and
computational apparatus, the Chern-Simons / Koide mass work, and a set of
falsifiable predictions, none of which are his.

Where this work succeeds or fails, that is the responsibility of the present
development, not of the original hypothesis. Crediting the intellectual origin is
not a claim that Gaede endorses this work, nor an endorsement of all of his
positions. It is simply the honest acknowledgment that the foundational idea —
and the philosophical stance that gives this programme its motivation — came from
him.
